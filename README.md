What Are Intra-Articular Injections?
Intra-articular injections are medications injected directly into a joint space to help reduce inflammation and relieve pain. These injections are commonly used to treat conditions affecting the:
•	Knee joints
•	Hip joints
•	Shoulder joints
•	Ankle joints
•	Wrist joints
Other joints affected by arthritis or inflammation
The most commonly used medications are corticosteroids, which are powerful anti-inflammatory agents designed to reduce swelling and irritation within the joint.
At Crystal Radiology, these procedures are performed under imaging guidance to ensure accurate placement of the medication into the affected joint.
How Do Intra-Articular Injections Help Joint Pain?
The primary purpose of intra-articular injections at Crystal Radiology is to reduce inflammation within the joint. Inflammation is often one of the main causes of pain, stiffness, and reduced mobility in patients with arthritis.
When the medication is delivered directly into the joint:
•	Swelling may decrease
•	Pain can be reduced
•	Joint movement may improve
•	Daily activities may become easier
•	Patients may experience improved quality of life
Because the medication is injected precisely where the inflammation exists, many patients notice symptom relief within days of treatment.
Who Benefits Most from Intra-Articular Injections?
Patients with mild to moderate osteoarthritis often experience the most significant benefit from steroid injections.
Research and clinical experience show that patients with:
•	Mild arthritis
•	Moderate arthritis
•	Inflammatory joint conditions
•	Localised joint pain
are more likely to experience meaningful pain relief compared to those with advanced or severe arthritis.
For many individuals, intra-articular injections at Crystal Radiology can provide a valuable treatment option when pain medications, physiotherapy, or lifestyle modifications have not provided sufficient relief.
How Long Does Pain Relief Last?
One of the most common questions patients ask is how long the effects of the injection will last.
The answer varies from person to person. Factors such as the severity of arthritis, overall joint health, activity levels, and individual response to treatment can all influence outcomes.
Generally:
•	Some patients experience relief for a few weeks.
•	Many patients report relief lasting 3 to 4 months.
•	Some patients may enjoy symptom improvement for up to 6 months.
While results vary, most patients experience at least some degree of pain reduction following treatment.
It's important to understand that the goal of the injection is symptom management rather than a permanent cure.
When Should You Consider an Intra-Articular Injection?
You may benefit from an injection if you experience:
•	Persistent joint pain
•	Arthritis-related stiffness
•	Difficulty walking or exercising
•	Joint inflammation
•	Limited improvement from conservative treatments
Your doctor may recommend intra-articular injections at Crystal Radiology as part of a broader treatment plan that may also include physiotherapy, exercise, weight management, and medication.
